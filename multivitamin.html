Explanation

Vitamin D reduces the chance of getting the flu, and magnesium reduces the chance of a heart attack. However, Vitamin D without Vitamin K2 leads to arterial calcification, so Vitamin K2 should be taken along with Vitamin D. Vitamin D and Vitamin K2 require cofactors including magnesium, zinc and vitamin A to work well. Organic magnesium is very bioavailable, but not magnesium oxide, which has a bioavailability of only 4%.

Therefore, to resist the flu and improve cardiovascular health, people should take a supplement with Vitamin D, Vitamin K2, and organic magnesium. Supplemental Vitamin K2 comes in the form of menatetrenone (MK-4) and/or menaquinone-7 (MK-7). An intake of 1,500 mcg/day of MK-4 is necessary to observably carboxylate osteocalcin, while only 45 to 90 (average of 67.5) mcg/day of MK-7 is necessary, so MK-7 is about 1,500 / 67.5 = 22 ~ 20 times more powerful. Therefore if a supplement contains Vitamin K2, but does not say what amount is in the form of MK-7, there is no way to know if it has enough MK-7 to have a significant effect. It should have at least 45 mcg of Vitamin K2 MK-7, more will have a greater protective effect, but also have a greater chance of causing adverse effects.
