Explanation

Vitamin D reduces the chance of getting the flu, and magnesium reduces the chance of a heart attack. However, Vitamin D without Vitamin K2 leads to arterial calcification, so Vitamin K2 should be taken along with Vitamin D. Vitamin D and Vitamin K2 require cofactors including magnesium, zinc and vitamin A to work well.

Organic magnesium is very bioavailable, but not magnesium oxide, which has a bioavailability of only 4%. So a multivitamin with calcium and magnesium oxide is only delivering calcium. Calcium without magnesium leads to arterial calcification, which leads to heart disease. Calcium without magnesium is especially bad for anyone with atrial fibrillation. The good multivitamins with organic magnesium, Vitamins D and K2 are available online, and sometimes in supplement stores. Unfortunately, a majority of the common brands have magnesium oxide, so there may not be any good multivitamins in a typical supermarket or pharmacy.

To resist the flu and improve cardiovascular health, people should take a supplement with Vitamin D, Vitamin K2, and organic magnesium. Supplemental Vitamin K2 comes in the form of menatetrenone (MK-4) and/or menaquinone-7 (MK-7). An intake of 1,500 mcg/day of MK-4 is necessary to observably carboxylate osteocalcin, while only 45 to 90 (average of 67.5) mcg/day of MK-7 is necessary, so MK-7 is about 1,500 / 67.5 = 22 ~ 20 times more powerful. Therefore if a supplement contains Vitamin K2, but does not say what amount is in the form of MK-7, there is no way to know if it has enough MK-7 to have a significant effect. It should have at least 45 mcg of Vitamin K2 MK-7, more will have a greater protective effect, but also have a greater chance of causing adverse effects.
