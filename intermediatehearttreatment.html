Multivitamins have calcium and magnesium, calcium is essential but if it is not balanced with a similar quantity of magnesium it leads to arterial calcification. When a multivitamin has magnesium oxide, the magnesium is not being absorbed in a similar quantity, so the person will get arterial calcification.

Vitamin K2 is necessary to move calcium from places it does not belong, like arteries, to places where it does belong, like bones and teeth, making bones strong and teeth resistant to cavities. EDTA binds to metals and is then excreted by the body. Linus Pauling and Matthias Rath found that vitamin C builds collagen, which strengthens blood vessels and prevents arterial calcification from forming. If you can't take vitamin C, other supplements to build collagen are collagen, chondroitin sulfate, glucosamine, and methylsulfonylmethane (MSM). If you can't take lysine, try proline.

The Pauling/Rath therapy includes at least 3 grams/day of both vitamin C and lysine. Since the therapy requires many supplements, some in high quantities, it is best to take them as powders if possible and mix them into a fruit drink which you would take with each meal. Like with the multivitamin, it is best to start with a small dose and slowly work up in case there are any adverse effects. So for a three day drink, start with 1/2 a teaspoon of both vitamin C and lysine, then increase that by 1/2 a teaspoon each week, until you get to 2 teaspoons, or just before you reach a dose which has adverse effects for you. A convenient way is to mix enough for three days in a glass bottle with a sleeve filled three quarters with juice, close and turn it over several times to mix it, then put that in the fridge, and drink some with each meal. It takes about 15 minutes to prepare the drink every three days, and time to buy the ingredients, so about 30 minutes every three days, or ten minutes a day.

With all the powders to mix and pills to take, this treatment takes around 10 minutes a day, and will greatly improve heart health.
